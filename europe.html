EUROPE

Europe is the world's second-smallest continent by surface area, covering about 7% of Earth's land, yet it is highly populous with over 40 countries. Renowned for its immense cultural, linguistic, and historical diversity, it is bordered by the Arctic, Atlantic, and Mediterranean. It houses the world's smallest nation (Vatican City) and largest (Russia).

Key Facts About Europe that houses the world's smallest nation (Vatican City) and largest (Russia).

Geography: Bordered by the Arctic Ocean (north), Atlantic Ocean (west), and Mediterranean Sea (south). It is technically a large peninsula of the Eurasian landmass. Population & Cities: Roughly 740 million people, making it the third most populous continent. Istanbul (Turkey) is the most populous city, partly in Europe and Asia. Landmarks & Nature: The Alps are the largest mountain system. The Volga is the longest river. It is home to active volcanoes like Mount Etna. Countries: Russia is the largest, while Vatican City is the smallest. Iceland is known for its green and safe environment, with Reykjavik as its northernmost capital. Culture & Lifestyle: Contains the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Known for diverse culinary traditions, such as Italian pizza and French cheese. Economy & Travel: France is the most visited country in the world. Europe is a major hub for tourism and international trade. Norway has the longest coastline.
